Ness — round 2

Four surviving directions, each with three variations. All marks are now framed in a squircle tile matching the original Harness icon. Yellow and gray pulled in throughout to widen the palette beyond pure green.
#22c55e green #fbbf24 amber #a3a3a3 / #737373 grays #0a0a0a tile
04 ETYMOLOGY
Headland
"Ness" = promontory. Land jutting into sea.
04a
Sunrise
Amber sun rising behind a gray distant ridge, green headland in the foreground. Adds depth and warmth.
04b
Three receding ridges
Gray, amber, green stacked as overlapping promontories. The sweep of the original Harness icon, applied to landscape.
04c
Beacon
Green coastline below, single amber beacon above the tallest peak with a soft glow. Faint gray reflection on the water.
04d
Shooting star
A yellow shooting star streaks left-to-right above the original 04 mountain. Below the horizon, a gray reflection of the star + trail mirrors it — same compression as the mountain reflection, but in gray so the warm streak is the original and the cool one is the echo.
05 HERITAGE
Dot grid
N picked out of the existing Harness icon's vocabulary.
05 BASELINE
All-green N
N picked out in pure green against dim gray background. Single amber accent in the top-right corner as a wink to the original icon.
05a
Diagonal status sweep
N cells colored by diagonal distance: gray (top-left, idle) → amber → green (bottom-right, done). Mirrors the original icon's left-to-right sweep.
05b
Posts green, diagonal amber
Each color carries meaning: posts (green) are the stable structure, diagonal (amber) is the active motion through. Cleanest read of the three.
05c
Rows tell the story
Top two rows gray (queued), middle two amber (working), bottom three green (done). Stage-by-stage progression.
06 STATUS DOTS
Status dots (no longer an N)
Other ways to use the existing dot vocabulary. No letterform.
06a
Three states, one row
Idle / working / done as three big dots. Directly translates the macOS traffic-light gesture into Harness's status vocabulary. Most concentrated read.
06b
Orchestrator + satellites
A green central node connected by thin threads to six satellites in mixed states — gray, amber, green. Reads as agent fan-out, the literal product picture.
06c
3×3 zoom of the heritage icon
Just nine dots in a clean diagonal sweep. Feels like a zoomed-in slice of the existing app icon — strong continuity, no letterform required.
06d
Yellow diagonal, gray cross-axis
Yellow descends the main diagonal, gray sits top-middle and bottom-middle, green fills the rest. More structural and less status-y than 06c — reads as a deliberate pattern, not a progress bar.
10 ABSTRACT
Wedge
No letterform. One or more diagonal blades.
10 BASELINE
Single green wedge
One tapered diagonal blade. Confident, quiet. Lets the wordmark do the naming.
10a
Trio: back to front
Three parallel wedges in depth: gray behind, amber middle, green leading. Reads as three agent lanes layered in z-space.
10b
Single wedge, three stripes
One blade split lengthwise into gray / amber / green bands. Same shape as the baseline, now carrying the status sweep inside it.
10c
Crossed
Bold green wedge with a thin amber wedge crossing the other direction over a faint gray wedge behind. Edges of an X — more energy, less calm than the others.
11 CREATURE
Loch Ness
The literal namesake. Nessie cresting the loch.
11
Silhouette, looking right
Green Loch Ness monster in silhouette — trailing humps and a skinny neck rising to a head that looks right. Solid yellow bands frame the top and bottom (10% each), each carrying a row of gray status dots like film-strip sprockets. A rippled, diffuse gray reflection dissolves into the water below.
